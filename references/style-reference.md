

# Style Reference

This document defines the preferred language patterns used in Elastic
sales enablement decks such as the APEX presentations.

When generating slide content, emulate the style used in these examples.

The goal is not to copy phrases directly, but to reproduce the
**structure, tone, and simplicity** of the language.

---

# Core Style Principles

The writing style should feel like an experienced seller explaining a
technology to another seller before a customer meeting.

Language should be:

• simple
• direct
• conversational
• concrete

Avoid:

• marketing language
• technical documentation tone
• vague business jargon
• long sentences

Slides should sound like something a seller could say naturally in a
conversation.

---

# Preferred Language Patterns

Use patterns such as:

"Teams struggling with…"

"Teams saying…"

"Helps teams…"

"Lets teams…"

"Most customers use this when…"

"A common problem is…"

"Works best when…"

"This usually comes up when…"

These patterns anchor the explanation in **real operational problems**.

---

# Good Style Examples

These examples represent the preferred tone for slide content.

Good:

"Teams saying incidents take too long to resolve."

"Security teams overwhelmed by alerts."

"Developers struggling to find the root cause of failures."

"Applications returning slow or irrelevant search results."

"Operations teams juggling too many monitoring tools."

"Organizations trying to consolidate search, observability, and security tools."

"Helps teams detect incidents faster and restore services quickly."

"Reduces investigation time by linking logs, metrics, and traces."

"Lets teams investigate security threats across all their data."

---

# Language to Avoid

Avoid phrasing that sounds like marketing copy or product documentation.

Avoid:

"Organizations seeking enhanced operational visibility."

"Provides a robust platform for enterprise-scale transformation."

"Enables advanced capabilities for digital modernization."

"Leverages innovative architecture to deliver unparalleled value."

"Provides a comprehensive end-to-end solution for enterprise workloads."

These phrases are vague and do not reflect how sellers speak.

---

# Compression Guidance

Slides are conversation prompts, not documentation.

Prefer:

Short sentences
One idea per bullet
Concrete language
Fewer syllables per word
Fewer words

Example:

Good:

"Teams saying incidents take too long to resolve."

Bad:

"Organizations experiencing challenges with incident management
and operational response workflows."

---

# Writing Target

When generating slide content, the language should feel like:

• a conversation between sellers
• practical advice from an experienced rep
• clear explanation of a customer problem

The writing should **sound human and practical**, not like marketing
or product documentation.
