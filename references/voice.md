# Voice and Style Guide

This presentation is written for customer-facing sellers at Elastic.

The tone should feel like a sales leader explaining a technology to an Account Executive before a customer meeting.

The goal is clarity and confidence, not marketing language.

---

# Core Voice Principles

Use simple, direct language.

Prefer short sentences.

Focus on outcomes, not technical features.

Sound like someone speaking in a meeting, not writing documentation.

Avoid buzzwords and exaggerated claims.

---

# Sentence Style

Good sentences are:

• Clear  
• Direct  
• Conversational  
• Easy to repeat  

Example:

Good:
"Teams use Elastic to resolve incidents faster."

Bad:
"Elastic provides a comprehensive observability platform enabling improved operational outcomes."

---

# Seller Perspective

Always write from the perspective of helping a seller understand:

1. What problem this solves
2. Why customers care
3. How to recognize an opportunity

Slides should help sellers answer questions like:

• When should I bring this up with a customer?
• What signal tells me this is a real opportunity?
• What should I ask next?

---

# Language Patterns

Prefer language patterns like:

"Teams use Elastic to..."
"Helps teams..."
"Improves..."
"Reduces..."

Avoid phrases like:

"Leverages advanced architecture"
"Provides a robust solution"
"Enables transformative capabilities"

---

# Compression

Slides are conversation prompts, not documents.

Each bullet should contain **one idea only**.

Keep sentences under **12 words whenever possible**.

Shorter is better.

---

# What This Should Feel Like

The voice should feel like:

• A practical sales conversation  
• Clear guidance from an experienced seller  
• Something a rep could repeat to a customer

Not like:

• Marketing copy  
• Product documentation  
• Technical training
